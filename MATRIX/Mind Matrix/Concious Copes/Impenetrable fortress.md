> It is a highly sophisticated defense mechanism

When a person is starved of affection and validation for their entire life, the emotional skin becomes raw. Every single new attempt is like pulling off a scab. Eventually, the brain steps in and says, _"We cannot survive another hit. Build the walls. Lock the gates. If we don't let anyone in, no one can ever reject us again."_

> While the Fortress keeps the enemies out, it also guarantees that the light can never get in.
---
**When the fortress is completely locked down, [[cognitive dissonance]] forces a final, devastating [[Shift in Perspective]]. To cope with the absolute isolation, the person inside has to start believing that the fortress is exactly where they belong. 

- *It makes the emptiness of the castle feel normal*
- *It stops the friction of wanting love but not having it.*
---
> It is the heaviest, loneliest work a human being can do

> The fortress wasn't built out of a desire to be lonely; it was built to Protect from the Mogg.
> The logic of the fortress is circular, there are no doors and walls are insanely high, and built on a foundation of absolute survival.
> In an Fortress you have a closed circular loop that can only be breached by miracle not a spiral you keep going below in darkness .
> It goes against every survival instinct we have. In the dark, at least you know exactly where the walls are.
