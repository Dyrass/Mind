# Before the reality Hit - ( Avoidance )
**preEmptive dissonance control** : they are fixing the gap before it even has a chance to form.
> people who never even try 
> if i have/could have / would have ........ 

They are master at tricking their brain . they plan ahead of any dissonances that they may face in the future and come up with Excuses and Explanations . they then search for outcomes which will happen (100% wahrscheinlichkeit) and either Delay that / procrastinate or change their course entirely. <font color="#00b050">Its a dual edge Sword.</font>
If you never attempt the task, you can never fail. And if you never fail, you never have to face the agonizing feeling of: _"<font color="#00b050">I gave this my absolute all, and I still wasn't good enough.</font>"_

**Destructive.** Traps you in a loop of never knowing your true potential.
## The Hidden Double Win

For the brain, self-handicapping is a win-win strategy, which is why it is so incredibly addictive:

1. **If they fail:** They have a perfect excuse. _"I didn't even try, so of course it didn't work."_ Their self-esteem remains completely intact because their actual ability was never tested.
2. **If they miraculously succeed:** They look like an absolute genius. _"Wow, I didn't even study/prepare and I still pulled it off!"_

# After the Reality hit - (Escapism)
### Usually.
- Numb the pain because they don't have the tools to fix the problem.
- Turning to substances or screen time to drown out the anxious feeling of the mental gap.
Doom-scrolling for six hours or drinking because you know your career is at a dead end.

**Destructive.** Buries the anxiety temporarily while the actual problem gets worse.

## The Bad Habits

Sometimes the mental friction causes actual, physical anxiety. If the brain can't fix the logic, it tries to drown out the emotion.

- **Addiction and Distraction:** Turning to substances, doom-scrolling, video games, or overeating. It doesn’t solve the gap between your thoughts and actions; it just numbs the brain so you temporarily forget the gap exists.
    
- **Anger and Outrage:** When confronted with an undeniable truth that breaks their worldview, people often lash out in anger at the person delivering the message. Anger is a powerful shield—it replaces the vulnerable, painful feeling of _confusion_ with the powerful, intense feeling of _certainty_.
