### 1. Chronic Hyper-Vigilance to "Fairness" (Warped Fairness)

Their brain has an internal ledger that tracks suffering. Because they were punished early in life and received no unconditional love, they learned a brutal lesson: _The world is a cruel, rigid place, and you only get safety by paying a heavy toll of pain and obedience._

- **How they think:** _<mark style="background:#fff88f">I paid my toll. I ate dirt for twenty years. That is the price of admission to life</mark>

- **The Glitch:** When they see you bypassing the toll booth—living freely without suffering the way they did—their brain perceives it as a **literal violation of the laws of physics**. It feels to them like you are stealing. Their obsession with rules isn't about loving the rules; it's a desperate demand that _everyone_ pay the same misery tax they did, otherwise the ledger is broken.
### 2. Splitting and Black-and-White Thinking
To survive their own mistreatment, they had to internalize a very simplistic, "teacher's pet" view of the world to keep their sanity:
- **The Good Path:** Obedience, compliance, suffering, keeping your head down.
- **The Bad Path:** Defiance, individuality, questioning authority, freedom.

Because they sacrificed their own desires to stay on the "Good Path," they _must_ believe that the "Bad Path" leads to ruin.
- **How they think:** When you take the "Bad Path" (breaking the norm) and you actually _thrive_, it triggers a massive wave of [[cognitive dissonance]] (the mental pain of holding two conflicting realities). Their brain cannot process the idea that a person can be defiant _and_ happy. To stop the mental pain, their mind instantly labels you as arrogant, selfish, or a cheater.
### 3. Externalized Self-Worth (The Missing Core)
Because they were never supported or loved just for who they were, they don't have an internal sense of self-worth. Their entire value is tied to **external validation**—getting the promotion, having the perfect lawn, following the protocol, or being praised by the authority figure.
- **How they think:** *I am worthy because I follow the script.*
- **The Glitch:** Since their worth is entirely external, it is incredibly fragile. They don't know how to validate themselves, so they judge their standing entirely by looking at the people around them. If you lift yourself up, they feel like they are sinking. They feel that for them to be "big," you _must_ be kept "small."
