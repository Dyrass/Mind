This is a **Konjunktiv II + Passiv** construction with a fronted object. Let me break down the architecture.

---

**The core pattern:**

`Was [subject] [Konjunktiv II modal] [verb], war [das Ding].`

---

**Layer by layer:**

**1. Relativsatz als Subjekt** — "Was sie hätten befürchten sollen"

This is a *was*-clause functioning as the grammatical subject of the whole sentence. It means roughly "that which...". The thing it describes gets revealed at the end after *war*.

**2. Doppelter Infinitiv** — "hätten befürchten sollen"

Konjunktiv II of a modal in a perfect construction always produces this double infinitive stack: `hätten + [main verb infinitiv] + [modal infinitiv]`. The modal here is *sollen* (obligation from outside — "should have"), and the main verb is *befürchten*.

The formula: `hätten/wären + [Infinitiv] + [Modal-Infinitiv]`

**3. War + Nominativ** — the reveal

*War* is the main verb connecting the *was*-clause (subject) to the thing being identified (predicate nominative). The structure is essentially: `[was-clause] = [das Ding]`.

---

**The skeleton:**

> Was sie hätten [Verb-INF] [Modal-INF], war [Nomen/Pronomen].

---

**Your own sentences using the same bones:**

- *Was er hätte sagen sollen, war die Wahrheit.*
- *Was wir hätten vermeiden sollen, war der Konflikt.*
- *Was du hättest verstehen müssen, war ihre Absicht.*
- *Was sie hätten mitbringen können, war Geduld.*

---

The key moves to internalize: *was* opens a subject clause, the double infinitive sits at the end of that clause (verb-final), then *war* pivots to the thing being named. Once you have the double infinitive order locked — `hätten + INF + Modal-INF` — you can slot any verb-modal pair into it.
