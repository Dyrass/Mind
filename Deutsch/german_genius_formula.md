## 🎯 The 3-Genius Framework

---

### 🎹 MOZART MODE — *Chunk the Music, Not the Notes*

Mozart never memorized individual notes. He heard **phrases, emotions, arcs**.

**Applied to German:**

- Never learn a lone word. Learn it inside a **felt sentence**.
  - ❌ `sprechen` = to speak
  - ✅ `Ich muss kurz mit dir sprechen.` ← freeze this. feel it. own it.

- When a sentence clicks → **STOP. Don't move on.**  
  Sit with it 10 seconds. Let your brain photograph it.  
  This is a chunk forming. Protect it.

- **The Mozart Rule:** 5 deeply felt chunks > 50 memorized words.

**ADHD hack:** Your brain loves pattern + novelty. German sentences have music — the verb kicks to the end, the stress lands weird. *Lean into that strangeness.* It's dopamine.

---

### ⚡ TESLA MODE — *Run the Simulation Inside*

Tesla never built a flawed prototype. He ran the machine in his mind until it was perfect.

**Applied to German:**

- After reading or listening — **close your eyes and replay it.**  
  Not translating. Just hearing it again internally. Let it echo.

- **The Inner Monologue Drill** (Tesla's core move):  
  Narrate your life in German. Not out loud. *Inside.*  
  - Walking to the kitchen: *Ich gehe in die Küche.*  
  - Feeling bored: *Mir ist langweilig gerade.*  
  No pressure. No grade. Just the simulation running.

- **Mental Editing:** Take a sentence you know. Change one part.  
  `Ich gehe ins Kino` → `Er geht ins Kino` → `Er ging ins Kino`  
  Feel if it sounds right. You're building grammatical *instinct*, not rule-following.

**ADHD hack:** This works perfectly for you because it costs zero external energy. You're lying down, zoning out — and learning. Your brain is already doing this with random thoughts. Redirect that engine.

---

### 🔥 BEETHOVEN MODE — *Obsession as a Feature, Not a Bug*

Beethoven was famously chaotic, impulsive, restless — and he channeled it into **deep, furious immersion** in short windows.

**Applied to German:**

- You will have days where German feels *electric*. Drop everything. Go deep.  
  Watch German YouTube, read a story, listen on loop. Ride the wave.  
  **These sessions do more than 2 weeks of disciplined studying.**

- On low-dopamine days → do ONE tiny thing only:  
  Read 1 sentence. Hear 1 word. That's it. You showed up. Dopamine meter ticks up.

- **Beethoven's obsession trick:** Find ONE German thing that genuinely interests you.  
  Football commentary. Dark humor. Memes. A show. A song.  
  Your brain will learn *around* that interest automatically.

**ADHD hack:** You cannot fake interest. Don't try. Find the real hook and let the language attach to it.

## 🧠 ADHD-Specific Rules That Cannot Be Broken

### The Dopamine Ladder
Start your German session with the *easiest* thing first.  
Kill the small enemy. Get the loot. Then climb.

```
Level 1 → Listen to 1 German sentence you already know
Level 2 → Read a short text (known vocab)
Level 3 → New chunk / new input
Level 4 → Mental simulation / inner monologue
```
Never start at Level 4. Your brain will panic and quit.

---

### The Spiral Rule
Your day starts in an upward or downward spiral before you even open a textbook.  
**Protect the morning.**

- No Reels/Shorts on waking → this fries receptors before German even gets a chance
- Drink water. Stretch. 5 minutes of quiet. *Then* open German.
- German works best when your dopamine meter is clean, not pre-burned.

---

### Environment = Output
Your brain becomes its environment.

| Environment | German Output |
|---|---|
| Noisy room, notifications on | Fragmented, frustrated, zero retention |
| Clean desk, phone away, one tab open | Chunks form, simulation runs, flow possible |

One app. One tab. One task.  
The more you context-switch while studying, the less German reaches long-term memory.

---

### Short Deadlines Only
- ❌ "I'll be B1 by end of year" → ADHD brain: *we have time, do it later*
- ✅ "I will read this paragraph in the next 7 minutes" → ADHD brain: *game on*

Give yourself micro-deadlines with small visible rewards after.

---

### Stimming is Allowed
Fidget while listening to German audio. Pace while doing inner monologue.  
Walk while replaying sentences.  
Movement sends the extra commands somewhere else → your mind clears → German lands better.

---

### Fear of Loss > Hope of Gain
Instead of: *"If I study I'll be fluent someday"*  
Think: *"If I skip today I lose the streak / the chunk fades"*

Frame each session as protecting something you already have.

---

## 📅 The Daily Formula (Minimum Effective Dose)

| Time | Action | Mode |
|---|---|---|
| Morning (5 min) | Replay 1–2 sentences from yesterday in your head | Tesla |
| Any idle moment | Inner monologue narration | Tesla |
| 1 focused burst (15–25 min) | New input: story / audio / video | Beethoven |
| After input (2 min) | Close eyes, let it echo. No new input. | Mozart |
| Before sleep | One German thing you enjoy (show, music) | Beethoven |

**Total active effort: ~25 minutes.**  
The rest is simulation — and that's where the real learning lives.
